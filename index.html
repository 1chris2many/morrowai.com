About

I'm not a consultant who used to build things. I build things every day and consult on the side.

My path to AI product leadership wasn't conventional. I spent 12 years in the U.S. Army — Ranger School, a deployment to Iraq, leading 180-person organizations, and earning recognition from the President. That's where I learned that strategy without execution is just a slide deck.

After my MBA at Vanderbilt, I joined Amazon and spent 7 years building AI-powered products. I created Alexa Shopping experiences, launched the Skill Flow Builder (adopted by EA, Sony Pictures, and Ernst & Young), and then founded Amazon Rufus — a GenAI conversational shopping assistant that went from zero to 120M monthly active users across 5 countries.

Now at Meta, I lead AI Search for Places — powering discovery across 210M+ locations globally. I've spoken at Ai4, Product School, GDC, and serve as a DeepLearning.ai Ambassador.

I consult because the problems are interesting, the stakes are real, and most companies deserve better advice than what they're getting from people who haven't shipped anything in years.
